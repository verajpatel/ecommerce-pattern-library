Chicken Egg

The most common egg used in human culture. Having many nutirents and vitamins, they are good for anything, ranging from breakfast to burgers.

Duck Egg

Similar to a chicken egg, but with a larger yolk. This egg has a more richer and smoother taste, plus with more fat and protein than a chicken's egg

Quail Egg

Size doesn't matter for these tiny eggs, because they have the same benefits of a chicken egg, and can pack a punch in cuisine. Some countries consider these to be a delicacy.

Goose Egg

This egg is double the size of a chicken egg. They have more protein inside them, but they're hard to crack open.

Turkey Egg

Similar to a duck's egg, but with a thicker yolk and egg white, giving it a more creamy taste. The richer flavor is often used more for pastries

Pheasant Egg

These are the size of a duck's egg with a light taste similar to a quail's egg. However, it's more gamey, as the pheasant is a gamey bird.

Ostrich Egg

Out of all the birds, the ostrich egg is the biggest egg ever laid! Ostrich Eggs are literally large hard-to-crack chicken eggs, but with a lot of calories packed in one shell.

{% pattern buttons/light %}

Emu Egg

These are some large eggs, but they're slightly less heavy than the ostrich. This dark shelled egg is one of the richest tasting eggs ever, with nothing to ooze out once cut open.

Dinosaur Egg

This prehistoric eggs have been aged over time to create a god-like flavor. Truly a rarity in modern cuisine.

Pokemon Egg

These are the result of breeding two Pocket Monsters together. Depending on the Pocket Monster Type, flavor, cooking time, health benefits, and what Pokemon egg it is are changing and different from one another.

Platypus Egg

Platypus Eggs are not the best eggs for consumption, due to the toxins that the platypus carry. However, if you want a prickly friend, then buying these eggs are the go-to option for making them.
